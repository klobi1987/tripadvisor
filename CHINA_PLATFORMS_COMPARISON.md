## 1. Dianping (大众点评) - "Chinese Yelp"

- **Western Equivalent:** Yelp
- **Founded:** 2003
- **Owner:** Meituan (merged 2015)
- **Users:** 600M+
- **Primary Use:** Restaurant reviews, local business discovery
- **Key Demographics:** All ages, urban focus

### Why It Matters for Restaurants
- **THE platform** for restaurant discovery in China
- Reviews heavily influence dining decisions
- Local search dominance
- Integration with delivery (Meituan)
- Price point and photo expectations

- Panorama: Most popular with 224 reviews - best value perception
- Dubravka: 153 reviews at budget-friendly ¥234 (€30)
- **Total: 566 reviews** across 3 restaurants
- Inverse correlation: Lower price = more reviews = more popular

### Key Insights
- **Value for money** is critical for Chinese tourists
- Price sweet spot: ¥250-300 (€33-40) for European fine dining
- Premium restaurants need different marketing (not just reviews)
- Photos of food, atmosphere, and views essential
- Responding to reviews in Chinese builds trust

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. **Google Translate for Chinese** ❌
- Results in embarrassing errors
- Damages credibility immediately
- **Solution:** Always use professional native speaker translation

### 2. **Focusing on Only One Platform** ❌
- Chinese users cross-reference multiple platforms
- Need consistent presence everywhere
- **Solution:** Multi-platform strategy with unified branding

### 3. **Ignoring Reviews and Comments** ❌
- Chinese expect engagement and responses
- Silence interpreted as not caring
- **Solution:** Respond to ALL reviews/comments within 24-48 hours in Chinese

### 4. **Poor Quality Photos** ❌
- Photography drives Chinese tourism
- Low quality photos = low quality restaurant perception
- **Solution:** Invest in professional photography, especially for Xiaohongshu

### 5. **No WeChat Pay/Alipay** ❌
- Many Chinese carry no cards or cash
- Creates friction and lost sales
- **Solution:** Integrate both payment systems immediately

### 6. **Treating Chinese Market as Add-on** ❌
- Requires dedicated strategy and resources
- Half-hearted efforts waste money
- **Solution:** Commit to proper investment or don't enter market

### 7. **Ignoring Cultural Differences** ❌
- Dining preferences differ from Western
- Service expectations different
- **Solution:** Train staff on Chinese guest preferences

### 8. **No Data Tracking** ❌
- Can't optimize without measurement
- Don't know what's working
- **Solution:** Track Chinese guests separately, measure ROI by channel
