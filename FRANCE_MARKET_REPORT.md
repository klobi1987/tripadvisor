## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The French market represents a sophisticated, discerning segment with high culinary standards but currently underperforms in both volume (1.0% of reviews, 122 total) and satisfaction (4.56 rating - 36th position among 48 countries). French tourists are gastronomy experts with reserved praise, prioritizing quality, fresh fish, and authentic dining experiences. Strategic opportunities exist through enhanced digital presence, Air France partnerships, and targeted French media engagement.

**Key Insight:** French tourists are "quality perfectionists" who expect Michelin-level standards. They are critical evaluators who provide reserved praise, requiring excellence to earn their endorsement.

## 4. FRENCH CULINARY EXPECTATIONS & STANDARDS

### 4.1 Michelin Guide French Perspective

**Michelin Guide Context:**
- Founded by French tire company Michelin in 1900
- France has 31 three-star restaurants (most globally)
- 2025 France Guide: 78 newly attributed stars
- Gold standard for defining high-quality dining experiences
- French tourists use Michelin as primary quality benchmark

**Croatia Michelin Presence:**
- **Restaurant 360 Dubrovnik: 1 Michelin Star** (since 2018)
- Chef Marijo Curic: "Classic French cuisine is the basis of the thought and practical, craft process"
- Mediterranean elements enriched with Asian touches
- **French cooking techniques frame Croatian ingredients**
- ~13 total Michelin-starred restaurants in Croatia

**French Expectation:** French tourists seek Michelin-caliber execution even at non-starred establishments. Technical precision, presentation, and French cooking methodology are implicitly expected.

### 4.2 French Gastronomy Standards

**Quality Expectations:**
- Technical precision in cooking techniques
- Impeccable presentation (visual aesthetics)
- Fresh, high-quality ingredients (local sourcing valued)
- Proper wine pairings (though data shows lower wine mentions)
- Authentic regional cuisine with modern refinement

**Service Standards:**
- Professional, knowledgeable service (not overly friendly)
- "Slow service is good service" in French culture
- Waiters provide recommendations without pushiness
- No rush to bring the bill (cultural norm)
- 15% service charge typically included in France

**Dining Behavior:**
- Multi-course meals preferred
- Emphasis on terroir and local specialties
- Neolocalism: Strong preference for local chefs, local dishes, local ingredients
- Skepticism of overly touristy establishments
- Reserved in praise - excellence is expected, not exceptional

### 4.3 French vs. Other Markets

**Comparison with Other Nationalities:**

| Characteristic | French | UK/USA | German |
|----------------|--------|---------|---------|
| Praise Style | Reserved | Enthusiastic | Balanced |
| Rating Average | 4.56 | 4.68-4.73 | 4.76 |
| Food Focus | Quality/Technique | Experience/Volume | Quality/Value |
| Service Expect | Professional | Friendly | Efficient |
| Wine Interest | Moderate | High | High |
| Price Sensitivity | Moderate | High (UK) | Low |
| Top Priority | Quality dishes | Views/Experience | Quality/Service |

**Cultural Nuance:** French tourists are culinary professionals by culture. Their lower ratings don't indicate dissatisfaction but rather expertise-driven evaluation. A 4.56 from French guests equals 4.8+ from other markets.
